Check what's right for you!!



age 



  • Energy (calories) should be adequate to support growth and development and to reach or maintain desirable body weight.
  • Eat foods low in saturated fat, trans fat, cholesterol, salt (sodium), and added sugars.
  • Keep total fat intake between 30 to 35 percent of calories for children 2 to 3 years of age and between 25 to 35 percent of calories.
  • About 2,000 to 2,400 calories are required each day.
  • Meal plan should consist of 10 ounces of grains, 2.5 cups of fruits, 3.5 cups of vegetables, 3 cups of dairy products, 7 ounces of protein foods such as meat, seafood, chicken, tofu, eggs, nuts and seeds, 8 teaspoons of oils and 400 additional calories per day.
  • the average active man can require more than 3,000 calories per day
  • adults should consume no more than 30% of total calories from fat (approximately 60 grams in a 2000 calorie diet) and a maximum of 10% of total calories as saturated fat (about 20 grams)
  • high consumption of foods containing omega 3 fats, most notably flax seeds, is believed to protect against prostate cancer.
  • Diet plans should focus on lean meats and proteins, whole grains, fresh fruits and vegetables, and unsaturated fats.
  • old man needs 7 oz. of grains – with at least half of these coming from whole grain sources – 3 cups of dairy, 6 oz. of protein, 2 cups of fruit and 3 cups of vegetables to provide balanced nutrition.
  • Pay attention to portion sizes, sticking to just a palm-size piece of meat and a cup of whole grains at most meals.
  • Serve whole-grain/high-fiber breads and cereals rather than refined grain products. Look for “whole grain” as the first ingredient on the food label and make at least half your grain servings whole grain.
  • Recommended grain intake ranges from 2 oz./day for a one-year-old to 7 oz./day.
  • Serve a variety of fruits and vegetables daily, while limiting juice intake. Each meal should contain at least 1 fruit or vegetable.
   
  • 1,800 to 2,400 calories per day are required depending on the activity level.
  • 45 to 65 percent of their calories from carbohydrates, 10 to 30 percent from protein and 25 to 35 percent of their daily calories from fats.
  • Healthy carbs include whole grains, legumes, fruits, vegetables, milk and yogurt; high-protein foods include lean meats, poultry, seafood, eggs, dairy products, tofu, seitan, legumes, nuts and seeds; and healthy fats include vegetable oils, purified fish oils, olives, avocados, nuts and seeds.
  • About 1,800 to 2,200 calories are required each day for healthy weight maintenance
  • A diet centered around organic vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes (particularly soy products), nuts, seeds, and wild-caught cold-water fish provides all of the necessary nutrients for health and disease prevention.
  • 2,000 to 2,200 calories per day are required to maintain a healthy body weight and to lead an active lifestyle.
  • plenty of complex carbohydrates, unsaturated fats, fruits, vegetables and foods high in fiber should be consumed
